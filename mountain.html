Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

About the Pose

The Mountain Pose, or Tadasana, is a foundational yoga posture that helps improve posture, balance, and focus. It may look simple, but it engages many muscle groups and can enhance awareness of the body and breath.

Conditions to Avoid

Individuals with the following conditions should avoid the Mountain Pose or consult a healthcare provider before practicing:

  • Severe balance issues
  • Low blood pressure
  • Recent knee, hip, or foot injuries

Restrictions

Ensure that you practice this pose on a flat, stable surface. Avoid locking your knees and maintain a natural, soft bend. Beginners may find it helpful to practice near a wall for additional support.

Benefits

  • Improves posture and body alignment
  • Enhances balance and stability
  • Strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles
  • Increases awareness and focus
  • Promotes a sense of calm and grounding
